Consider the “base” Lie algebra $\overset{\circ}{\mathfrak{g}}$ with nondegenerate invariant bilinear form $(\cdot|\cdot)$. Take ${u_i},{u^{i}}$ dual basis. We have Casimir operator

\[\overset{\circ}{\Omega}=\sum u_iu^i.\]

We know that the Casimir operator is independent w.r.t. the choice of basis. Then, in particular,

\[\overset{\circ}{\Omega}=\sum u_iu^i=\sum u^iu_i\implies \sum_i [u_i,u^i]=0.\]

And this proves that

\[\sum_i [u_i^{(m)},u^{i(n)}]=\sum_i [u_i,u^i]^{(m+n)}+m\delta_{m,-n}K=m\delta_{m,-n}(\dim \overset{\circ}{\mathfrak{g}})K.\]

Now, recall that we have defined the generalized Casimir operator acts on reduced module

\[\Omega=2(K+\check{h})d+\overset{\circ}{\Omega}+2\sum_{n=1}^\infty \sum_i u_i^{(-n)}u^{i(n)}.\]
